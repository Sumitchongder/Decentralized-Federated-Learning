# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This project is dedicated to advancing decentralized, privacy-preserving
federated learning through open research and engineering excellence.

We are committed to fostering a **professional, respectful, and inclusive**
environment for all contributors, users, and reviewers, regardless of
background, expertise, or affiliation.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and constructively
- Engage in technical discussions with evidence and clarity
- Respect differing architectural, cryptographic, or research viewpoints
- Follow ethical research and responsible disclosure practices
- Uphold academic integrity and open-source professionalism

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of research or code
- Deliberate introduction of vulnerabilities or malicious code
- Publishing private keys, sensitive data, or confidential materials
- Disruptive or misleading conduct in issues or pull requests

---

## Research & Engineering Integrity

Given the security-sensitive and research-driven nature of this repository:

- Experimental results must be reproducible or clearly labeled as exploratory
- Security claims must be substantiated or scoped appropriately
- Cryptographic changes must follow best practices and standards
- Blockchain and consensus logic must be carefully reviewed

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers reserve the right to:

- Remove content that violates this Code of Conduct
- Reject or revert contributions that compromise integrity or safety
- Restrict participation in cases of repeated or severe violations

---

## Reporting Concerns

Concerns about behavior, integrity, or conduct may be reported privately
via GitHub or directly to the project maintainer.

All reports will be handled confidentially and fairly.

---

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is inspired by the Contributor Covenant and adapted
for decentralized systems, security-focused, and research-oriented projects.
